Peter Parker's Spider-Man


How did Peter Parker became Spider-Man?


Peter Parker became Spider-Man when a special spider bit him during a science show. This wasn’t just any spider—
it had been changed by science and gave Peter superpowers! After the bite, he could climb walls, jump really high,
and sense danger like a real spider. That’s how he turned into Spider-Man! 🕸️🕷️

What Power Does Spiderman Have?


Spider-Man has some amazing powers that make him a super cool superhero! After being bitten by a special spider,
Peter Parker gained the ability to stick to walls and ceilings, just like a real spider. He also became super strong and
super fast, able to jump high and move quickly to dodge danger. One of his coolest powers is his “spider-sense,”
which gives him a tingly feeling when something bad is about to happen. Besides those powers, Spider-Man can
shoot webs from his wrists using gadgets he built himself. He uses these webs to swing between buildings, catch
bad guys, and save people in trouble. All of these powers help him protect his city and make him one of the most
loved superheroes around! 🕸️🕷️

Some Notable Villains That Spider-Man Had Fought?


Spider-Man has faced lots of famous villains over the years! Here are some of the most notable ones:

  • Green Goblin (Norman Osborn): A super-scary villain with pumpkin bombs and a flying glider. He’s one of Spider-Man’s most dangerous enemies
  • Doctor Octopus (Otto Octavius): A brilliant scientist with four powerful metal arms that can crush anything.
  • Venom: A creepy alien creature called a symbiote that bonds with people and gives them powers like Spider-Man’s—but much scarier!
  • Mysterio: A master of illusions who tricks people with special effects and holograms. These villains make Spider-Man’s job super tough—but he
    always finds a way to swing back! 🕸️ Want to know which ones appear in the movies or cartoons?
